Mandarin Numbers
一

yī (一) — "One". The beginning, unity, or singularity.

二

èr (二) — "Two". Used for counting; represents duality.

三

sān (三) — "Three". Symbolizes harmony and balance.

四

sì (四) — "Four". Sounds like "death" (死 sì) in Chinese, considered unlucky.

五

wǔ (五) — "Five". Symbol of balance (like five elements, directions).

六

liù (六) — "Six". Associated with smoothness and good luck.

七

qī (七) — "Seven". Represents togetherness and sometimes mystery.

八

bā (八) — "Eight". Very lucky in Chinese culture (sounds like wealth).
